## Suggested structure

Walk through the lifecycle: from first contact to delivery and beyond. Be
concrete about timelines, deliverables, and what you need from the customer.

## Example

### 1. Discovery (1–2 weeks)

A short paid sprint. We map requirements, sketch the architecture, and
produce a fixed-scope estimate for the build phase.

**You provide:** business goals, existing assets, stakeholder access
**You get:** scope document, technical architecture, delivery plan, quote

### 2. Design (2–3 weeks)

UX flows → wireframes → high-fidelity UI → design system. Everything is
reviewed and signed off before any production code is written.

### 3. Build (varies by scope)

Two-week sprints. Demo at the end of every sprint. Feedback gets prioritized
into the next sprint. Continuous deployment to a staging URL.

### 4. Launch & Handover (1 week)

Production deploy, monitoring setup, runbook, and a 30-day warranty period
for any defects.

### 5. Ongoing (optional)

Retainer for maintenance, feature work, or DevOps. Month-to-month, no
long-term lock-in.
